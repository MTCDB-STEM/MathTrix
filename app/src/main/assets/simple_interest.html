Key Concepts:

Formula: Remember the golden rule, SI = PRT/100, where SI is simple interest, P is principal, R is interest rate (decimal), and T is time (in years).

Units: Make sure all units are consistent. Convert percentages to decimals and years to fractions if needed.

Rate: If a sum doubles in "T" years. the rate is 100/T%. Comparison: For long periods, compound interest beats simple interest significantly.

Estimating: Roughly, simple interest is "principal x time x half the rate in years".

Shortcuts: Use online calculators or memorize common rates and times for quick caculations

Applications of Simple Interest:

Simple interest, often seen as a dusty arithmetic relic, actually pops up in various everyday situations! Here are some practical uses:

1. Borrowing and Lending

Loans: Understanding simple interest helps you compare loan options (personal loans, car loans, etc.). You can calculate the total interest paid and choose the loan with the lowest cost. Savings Accounts: While rare, some basic savings accounts offer simple interest. Knowing how much you'll earn helps plan you financial goals.
